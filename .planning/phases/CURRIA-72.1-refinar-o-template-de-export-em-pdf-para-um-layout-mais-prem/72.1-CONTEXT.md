# Phase 72.1 Context

## Goal

Refine the exported PDF resume template into a more premium, executive-looking layout while keeping the document ATS-safe, single-column, and visually aligned with the in-product preview through the same Inter font family.

## Problem

The PDF export was functional and parseable, but it still looked like a dry technical export: Helvetica-based typography, blunt separators, dense experience blocks, and little visual rhythm compared with the product preview.

## Decisions

- Keep the export strictly single-column and text-first to protect ATS parsing.
- Reuse the preview font family by embedding Inter in the PDF generator instead of switching to a new export-only font.
- Improve perceived quality through typography, spacing, section hierarchy, and experience rhythm rather than risky visual constructs.
- Keep the work scoped to PDF only; do not re-open DOCX, export persistence, ATS scoring, or resume content logic.
